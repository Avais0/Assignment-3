Exploring the World of Literature

Classic novels have had a profound and lasting impact on literature, shaping not only the development of narrative forms but also the way in which society views itself. Works like Don Quixote Moby-Dick Pride and Prejudice, and Crime and Punishment have become cornerstones of literary tradition, offering timeless themes of identity, morality, and the complexities of human nature. These novels have influenced generations of writers, providing models of structure, character development, and language that continue to resonate today. Through their exploration of universal themes—love, ambition, injustice, and the search for meaning—classic novels have helped to define the boundaries of literature, setting standards for artistic achievement and offering readers a deep, often transformative engagement with the world. Their continued relevance in modern culture underscores the enduring power of great storytelling to transcend time and place.

  5. "The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald
